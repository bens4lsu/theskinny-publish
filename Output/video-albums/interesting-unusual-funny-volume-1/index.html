Clearing Brush

Our rental house on Lake George had a serious problem. Overgrown trees and bushes blocked almost all view of the water from the house. The best angle was a lost cause, but there was the potential to see this little gravel beach with just a bit of a trim.

Fortunately for us, it got more than just a bit of a trim. What would you think if you had a vacation renter do this much landscaping?

My favorite part of this video is that it makes me laugh in real life exactly where it made me laugh when it was being shot.

Sunset Over National Harbor

We started out with a priceline room with the worst possible view. We didn't know it was the hotel assistant GM who had brought up our luggage, but he responded when Heather opened the window and said, "Well, that sucks."

I should have been snapping pics on the evening of the 22nd. The sunset was much prettier, and we would have caught a tall ship that passed and had the drawbridge open so that it could go through. Not much really happened the evening that I was set up, but it's kind of cool anyway.

BTW, this is my first experiment making a time lapse video. I wish I had taken more pics, to smooth it out a little, and I thought the sky would be more interesting, but in watching it, a smoother capture of the coming and going of boats would have been better.

Recorded: June 22, 2011

Duration: 00:08

Slowest Sailboat Race Ever

On June 1, 2011, we left work early, drove to Slidell, went out into the lake, and got ready to race. Everyone sat and waited for the marks to be placed, but they couldn't be placed at all. It's impossible to find a spot for an upwind mark when there is absolutely no wind at all.

We sat out there for close to an hour before the race was called on count of heat and boredom. Summer was officially on the lake.

The Christmas God-Awful PVC Ferris Wheel

We were taken to this unbelievable Christmas decorating display some 30 minutes south of Birmingham, AL. This is a glimpse of one feature, but there are hundreds and hundreds of items spread over an acre. The scene blazes with lights as little fake people celebrate Christmas in any way you can imagine. Clark Grizwold would weep with envy.

City Park Carousel

I love the carousel in New Orleans's City Park. It's one of the biggest and one of the fastest that I know, and it's ancient beams, bars, and mechanics reek of days past. The whole room is beautiful, and confusingly symmetric.

This is just a 17-second clip showing its canopy, and then coming down into the animals at the very end.
